# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

A checklist for responsible and smart social media use during your college years.

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do | Why It Matters |
|---|-----|----------------|
| 1 | **Think before you post** — pause and ask yourself if you'd be comfortable with a professor, parent, or future employer seeing it. | Digital content is permanent and can resurface years later. |
| 2 | **Keep your privacy settings updated** — regularly review who can see your profile, posts, and personal information. | Prevents strangers, scammers, or unwanted contacts from accessing your data. |
| 3 | **Use social media to network professionally** — connect with professors, alumni, internship coordinators, and peers in your field. | Platforms like LinkedIn can open doors to internships and job opportunities. |
| 4 | **Fact-check before sharing** — verify information from credible sources before reposting news or claims. | Sharing misinformation can damage your credibility and mislead others. |
| 5 | **Be kind and respectful in all interactions** — treat others online the way you would in person. | Cyberbullying has real consequences — academic, legal, and personal. |
| 6 | **Log out of shared devices** — always sign out after using campus computers, libraries, or dorm shared screens. | Protects your accounts from unauthorized access. |
| 7 | **Report harmful or abusive content** — use platform tools to flag harassment, hate speech, or dangerous misinformation. | Helps keep online spaces safer for everyone. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Don't | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|----------------|
| 1 | **Don't post photos or details of risky or illegal activities** — this includes underage drinking, drug use, or vandalism. | These posts can lead to disciplinary action, legal trouble, or lost job offers. |
| 2 | **Don't share your location in real time** — avoid tagging your exact location while still there, especially late at night. | Sharing live locations can compromise your personal safety. |
| 3 | **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying** — avoid targeting others online, and speak up or report when you witness it. | Cyberbullying causes serious mental health harm and can have legal consequences. |
| 4 | **Don't overshare personal information** — avoid posting your phone number, home address, student ID, or financial details. | This information can be exploited for identity theft or scams. |
| 5 | **Don't use social media as your only news source** — avoid relying on feeds or viral posts for important information. | Algorithms show you what's engaging, not necessarily what's accurate or balanced. |
| 6 | **Don't post in anger or frustration** — avoid venting about classmates, professors, or institutions in a public forum. | Impulsive posts can damage relationships, reputation, and academic standing. |
| 7 | **Don't ignore your mental health** — don't let social media comparison, FOMO, or toxic interactions go unchecked. | Excessive or unhealthy social media use is linked to anxiety and depression in college students. |

---

## 💡 Quick Reminder

> **The "Front Page Test":** Before posting anything, ask yourself —  
> *"Would I be okay seeing this on the front page of my college newspaper?"*  
> If not, don't post it.

---

*Use social media as a tool — for learning, connecting, and growing — not as a space that works against your future.*
