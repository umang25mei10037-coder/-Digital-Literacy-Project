       STAY SAFE ONLINE — CYBERCRIME PREVENTION CHECKLIST
      			Task 5 Part-B

================================================================


GENERAL ONLINE SAFETY
-----------------------

    TIP 1 — Use Strong, Unique Passwords for Every Account
    Create passwords with at least 12 characters using a mix of
    uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
    Never reuse the same password across multiple platforms.
    Use a trusted password manager (e.g., Bitwarden or Google
    Password Manager) to store them securely.

    TIP 2 — Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) Everywhere
    Turn on 2FA for your email, social media, and banking apps.
    Even if someone steals your password, they cannot access your
    account without the second verification step (OTP or
    authenticator app). Go to Settings > Security on each app
    and enable it today.

    TIP 3 — Never Click Links in Unsolicited Messages
    Do not click any link received via WhatsApp, SMS, or email
    from an unknown sender — even if it looks official. Scammers
    create fake pages that mimic government portals, banks, or
    universities. Always type the official URL directly into your
    browser instead.

    TIP 4 — Keep Your Phone and Apps Updated
    Enable auto-updates on your phone and all installed apps,
    especially banking and UPI apps. Software updates patch
    known security vulnerabilities. Outdated apps are one of
    the most common entry points for malware and data theft.

    TIP 5 — Be Careful About What You Share on Social Media
    Never publicly post your phone number, Aadhaar number, date
    of birth, or home address on any social platform. Cybercriminals
    use this information to craft targeted phishing attacks or
    attempt identity theft against you.

    TIP 6 — Avoid Using Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Transactions
    Never use unsecured public Wi-Fi (at cafes, railway stations,
    or malls) for UPI payments, net banking, or logging into
    sensitive accounts. Attackers can intercept data on open
    networks. Use your mobile data or a trusted VPN instead.


FINANCIAL & UPI SAFETY  [★ Required Tips]
------------------------------------------

    TIP 7 ★ — NEVER Share Your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV — EVER
    Your UPI PIN is only required when YOU are sending money.
    No bank, government body, scholarship portal, or company
    will ever ask for your PIN or OTP to send money TO you.
    If anyone asks — it is a SCAM. Hang up and block immediately.
    Remember: entering your PIN on a "collect request" sends
    money OUT of your account, it does NOT receive money.

    TIP 8 ★ — Verify All UPI Collect Requests Before Accepting
    On apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, or Paytm, a "Collect
    Request" DEBITS your account — it does not credit it.
    Scammers send fake collect requests disguised as refunds,
    prizes, or scholarship credits. Always REJECT collect
    requests from unknown numbers or unverified sources.
    When in doubt — decline and verify through official channels.


DEVICE & ACCOUNT SAFETY
--------------------------

    TIP 9 — Set a Daily UPI Transaction Limit in Your Bank App
    Most banks allow you to cap the maximum amount transferable
    per day via UPI. Set a low limit (e.g., Rs. 5,000-10,000)
    to minimise financial damage if your account is ever
    compromised. You can raise it temporarily when needed.

    TIP 10 — Always Log Out of Shared or College Devices
    Never save passwords or stay logged in on lab computers,
    library systems, or a friend's device. Always use private
    or incognito mode when accessing accounts on shared devices.
    Forgetting to log out gives full access to the next user.

    TIP 11 — Check QR Codes Carefully Before Making Payments
    Always verify the recipient name shown on your UPI app
    screen before entering your PIN at shops or canteens.
    Fraudsters sometimes place fake QR codes over legitimate
    merchant codes. If the name looks wrong or unfamiliar,
    stop and verify with the shopkeeper before proceeding.

    TIP 12 — Lock Your Phone and Enable Remote Wipe
    Set a strong PIN or biometric lock on your phone. Enable
    "Find My Device" on Android or "Find My iPhone" on iOS.
    If your phone is lost or stolen, you can remotely lock
    or erase it before anyone can access your banking or
    UPI apps. Do this now before it is too late.


================================================================
  IF YOU ARE A VICTIM — REPORT IMMEDIATELY
  (Acting fast, ideally within 1 hour, can help freeze funds)
================================================================

      STEP 1 — Call Helpline 1930 (National Cyber Crime Helpline)
              Available 24/7 nationally. Can initiate a bank
              freeze request to stop further fund transfers.

     STEP 2 — File a Complaint Online:
              cybercrime.gov.in
              File for all cybercrimes — UPI fraud, phishing,
              cyberbullying, identity theft — any time, 24/7.

     STEP 3 — Call Your Bank's Fraud Helpline
              Request an immediate transaction dispute and
              account hold to limit further damage.

     STEP 4 — File an FIR at Your Nearest Cyber Police Station
              Required for significant financial losses and
              essential for any legal follow-up or recovery.
