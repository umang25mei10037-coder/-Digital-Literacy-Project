## ✅ General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
  Create passwords with at least 12 characters mixing uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager (like Bitwarden or Google Password Manager) — never reuse the same password across platforms.

- [ ] **2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**
  Turn on 2FA for email, social media, and banking apps. Even if someone steals your password, they cannot access your account without the second verification step (OTP or authenticator app).

- [ ] **3. Never click on links in unsolicited messages.**
  Verify the sender before clicking any link received via WhatsApp, SMS, or email — even if it appears to be from a bank, government portal, or university. Go directly to the official website by typing the URL in your browser.

- [ ] **4. Keep your phone and apps updated.**
  Software updates patch security vulnerabilities. Enable auto-updates on your phone, browser, and apps — especially banking and UPI apps. Outdated apps are common entry points for malware.

- [ ] **5. Be cautious about what you share on social media.**
  Avoid publicly posting your phone number, date of birth, Aadhaar details, or home address. Scammers use this information to craft targeted phishing attacks or steal your identity.

- [ ] **6. Use only trusted, secured Wi-Fi networks.**
  Avoid using public Wi-Fi (cafes, railway stations, malls) for banking, UPI transactions, or logging into sensitive accounts. If necessary, use a VPN. Attackers can intercept data on unsecured networks.

## 💳 Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

- [ ] **7. ★ NEVER share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone — ever.**
  Your UPI PIN is only required when YOU are sending money. No bank, government body, scholarship portal, or company will ever ask for your PIN or OTP to send money to you. If someone asks, it is a scam — hang up immediately.

- [ ] **8. ★ Verify payment requests before accepting on UPI apps.**
  On apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, or Paytm, "Collect Requests" (money requests) debit YOUR account — not credit it. Scammers send fake collect requests disguised as refunds or prizes. Always reject unknown payment requests.

- [ ] **9. Set daily UPI transaction limits in your banking app.**
  Most banks allow you to set a daily cap on UPI payments. Keeping this limit low (e.g., ₹5,000–₹10,000) reduces your financial exposure if your account is ever compromised.

- [ ] **10. Check the QR code or UPI ID carefully before paying.**
  Fraudsters sometimes replace legitimate merchant QR codes with fake ones at shops or hostels. Always verify the recipient name shown on screen before entering your PIN.

## 📱 Device & Account Safety

- [ ] **11. Lock your phone with a strong PIN/biometric and enable remote wipe.**
  Enable "Find My Device" on Android or iPhone. If your phone is stolen, you can remotely lock or erase it before a scammer can access your UPI or banking apps.

- [ ] **12. Log out of accounts on shared/college devices.**
  Never save passwords or stay logged in on lab computers, shared laptops, or a friend's device. Always use private/incognito mode when accessing accounts on shared devices.

## 🚨 If You Are a Victim — Report Immediately

> Acting within the first few hours dramatically increases the chance of freezing funds and recovering money.

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in) — File an online complaint 24/7 for all cybercrimes including UPI fraud, phishing, cyberbullying, and identity theft |
| 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — Call immediately after any financial cybercrime. Available nationally. The helpline can initiate a bank freeze request to stop further transfers. |
| 🏦 **Your Bank's Fraud Helpline** | Call your bank immediately and request a transaction dispute / account hold |
| 🚔 **Local Cyber Police Station** | Visit your nearest police station and file an FIR if the amount is significant |
